# Jenkins User Access Review Plan

**Department:** IT CloudOps & Automation
**Frequency:** Semi-Annual (every 6 months)
**Purpose:** To ensure that Jenkins user access aligns with current organizational roles, follows the principle of least privilege, and meets security and compliance requirements.

---

## 1. **Scope**

This plan covers all users with access to the organization’s Jenkins environment, including:

* Employees in IT CloudOps & Automation
* Developers, QA, and operations staff with Jenkins accounts
* Contractors with temporary access
* Service accounts or automated integrations

**Access Types Covered:**

* Admin users
* Build/Execute users
* Read-only users
* Plugin/service accounts

---

## 2. **Objectives**

1. Verify that user access is still required based on current roles and responsibilities.
2. Remove or adjust access for users who no longer need it (e.g., transferred, promoted, left organization).
3. Ensure principle of least privilege is enforced.
4. Document findings and actions in Confluence for compliance and audit purposes.
5. Establish a repeatable, efficient, and semi-automated review process.

---

## 3. **Roles and Responsibilities**

| Role                              | Responsibility                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **IT CloudOps & Automation Team** | Conduct user access review, adjust permissions, document outcomes.                                   |
| **Department Heads / Managers**   | Provide guidance when role ambiguity arises; validate critical users’ access if required.            |
| **ISD / Security Team**           | Review the access review plan, ensure compliance with security standards, audit final documentation. |

---

## 4. **Review Methodology**

### Step 1: Extract Current User List

* Export Jenkins user list including: username, full name, email, last login, and current permission level.
* Identify inactive users (e.g., no login in last 90–180 days).

### Step 2: Categorize Users

* **Active** – Currently require access.
* **Inactive / Stale** – No login in the last 90–180 days.
* **Orphaned / Unknown** – Users who cannot be matched to current employees.

### Step 3: Validate Access Requirements

* Check HR system or internal employee directory to determine:

  * Users who have left or migrated to another department
  * Users who have been promoted or whose responsibilities changed
* Use practical heuristics where contacting every department is not feasible:

  * Focus on users with admin privileges first
  * Prioritize users with high-impact roles or access to critical pipelines

### Step 4: Adjust Permissions

* Remove users who no longer require access.
* Reduce privileges where possible to enforce least privilege.
* Temporarily disable accounts where clarification is pending.

### Step 5: Document in Confluence

* For each review cycle, create a Confluence page capturing:

  * Date of review
  * User list before and after review
  * Actions taken (removed, downgraded, retained)
  * Rationale for retaining elevated access
* Include a summary table and optional metrics (e.g., total users removed, percentage of users reviewed).

---

## 5. **Tools & Automation Recommendations**

* Use Jenkins API or CLI to export users and roles.
* Maintain a CSV or spreadsheet as a staging area for review before applying changes.
* Optional: Implement scripts to identify inactive users and automatically flag them for review.
* Use Confluence templates to standardize documentation.

---

## 6. **Frequency & Scheduling**

* **Review Frequency:** Semi-annual (every 6 months)
* **Proposed Timeline:**

  * **Month 1:** Extract and categorize user list
  * **Month 2:** Validate access and remove unnecessary accounts
  * **Month 3:** Document findings and submit to ISD for record

**Ad-hoc Review:** Triggered by major organizational changes, e.g., mass layoffs, team restructuring, or critical security incidents.

---

## 7. **Governance & Compliance**

* Follow principle of least privilege and role-based access controls (RBAC).
* Ensure auditability by maintaining Confluence records.
* Retain historical records of all access reviews for a minimum of 1 year.
* Escalate exceptions to ISD / Security Team if a user’s access cannot be validated.

---

## 8. **Key Considerations / Best Practices**

* Prioritize admin accounts and critical pipelines.
* Implement a semi-automated review workflow to reduce manual overhead.
* Avoid contacting every department individually; rely on HR directory data and last login heuristics.
* Encourage employees and managers to report outdated access proactively.

---

## 9. **Success Metrics**

* 100% of users reviewed for every cycle
* Inactive or orphaned accounts removed promptly
* Reduction in unnecessary admin-level users
* Confluence documentation completed and auditable
