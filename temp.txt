As part of the design and planning activities for the Nexus Repository v3.93.2 upgrade and migration, further review of the Sonatype architecture and database requirements was performed to validate the proposed PostgreSQL deployment.

During this review, it was clarified that the current ~3 TB storage reported by Nexus corresponds to the blob store (repository artifacts such as JARs, packages, Docker layers, etc.) and is separate from the PostgreSQL database, which stores Nexus application data (repository configuration, components, assets, users, roles, and related metadata).

Based on this clarification, the previously discussed 4 TB (expandable to 8 TB) storage requirement applies to the application server storage (blob store) rather than the PostgreSQL database. The PostgreSQL database sizing will be determined separately in coordination with the DBA team based on the application's metadata workload and Sonatype deployment recommendations.

This review has been documented to ensure the infrastructure requests submitted to the OS and DBA teams accurately reflect the application architecture and vendor guidance before provisioning activities commence.
